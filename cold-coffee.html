Cold Coffee

Cold Coffee is a refreshing, chilled beverage that transforms the sharp, bitter kick of coffee into a smooth, creamy, and indulgent treat. While it is enjoyed globally in various forms, the classic café-style version is loved for its perfect balance of robust espresso notes, creamy milk, and a thick, frothy top.

What Makes it Great

A well-made cold coffee relies on a simple yet satisfying blend of elements:

  • The Coffee Base: Typically made using a strong instant coffee powder dissolved in a tiny splash of warm water, or a freshly brewed, cooled shot of espresso.
  • The Creaminess: Chilled, full-cream milk is highly preferred. Blending the milk at high speed with the coffee builds a thick, airy layer of foam at the top.
  • The Sweetness: Granulated sugar, simple syrup, or condensed milk is added to round out the natural bitterness of the coffee beans.
  • The Presentation: Cafés often line the inside of the serving glass with chocolate syrup before pouring the coffee, giving it an artisanal, marbled look.

Ingredients

  • Coffee: 1 to 1.5 tablespoons of instant coffee powder (or 1 shot of freshly brewed espresso, cooled down)
  • Milk: 1.5 cups of whole milk (make sure it is ice-cold right out of the fridge for the best froth)
  • Sugar: 2 tablespoons of granulated sugar (adjust to your preference)
  • Warm Water: 1 to 2 tablespoons (only needed if using instant coffee, just to dissolve the powder)
  • Ice Cubes: 4 to 5 cubes (crucial for getting that thick, whipped texture)

Prepration Method

  1. Dissolve the Coffee (1 min):

    In a small cup or directly in your blender jar, mix the instant coffee powder, sugar, and warm water. Stir or shake for a few seconds until the coffee and sugar are completely dissolved into a smooth, dark syrup.

  2. Decorate the Glass (Optional) (30 secs):

    Take a tall serving glass and tilt it slightly. Drizzle the chocolate syrup around the inside rim while rotating the glass to create beautiful swirls. Place the glass in the freezer for 2-3 minutes to set while you blend the coffee.

  3. Add the Dairy & Ice (1 min):

    Pour the ice-cold milk into the blender jar along with the coffee syrup. Drop in the ice cubes. If you want a thicker, dessert-style drink, add a scoop of vanilla ice cream right now.

  4. Blend at High Speed (1-2 mins):

    Secure the blender lid and blend the mixture on high speed for 1 to 2 minutes. You will hear the ice cubes crushing, and the liquid will transform into a thick, pale brown mix with a heavy layer of micro-foam forming at the top.

  5. Pour and Serve (30 secs):

    Take your decorated glass out of the freezer. Pour the cold coffee slowly into the glass. Use a spoon to scoop out any extra velvety foam remaining at the bottom of the blender and pile it on top. Dust with a tiny pinch of cocoa powder or coffee powder, and enjoy immediately!
