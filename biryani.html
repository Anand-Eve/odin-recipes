Chicken Biryani

Biryani: Love of the Royals

Chicken Biryani is a highly celebratory Indian dish. A masterful harmony of aromatic, long-grain basmati rice, tender spiced chicken, and a complex blend of warm aromatics . More than just a chicken-and-rice dish, true biryani is all about texture and layered flavor.

Chicken biryani is one of India's most popular dishes, and it is believed that the Mughals were the first to introduce this delicious and flavorful dish in this country. The reason why chicken biryani is so famous is due to the different flavours and the ingredients it carries as well as the nostalgia it evokes as a royal and iconic dish. One of the most common ways to prepare chicken biryani is to layer spiced chicken followed by rice, fried onions, and ghee.

Some of the popular biryanis across India are:

  • Lucknowi Biryani - It is prepared in a copper handi , where chicken is cooked partially with a layer of rice at the top and then the pot is sealed wherein the heat inside the pot cooks the biryani.
  • Kolkata Biryani - It is a light spiced biryani with a little rose water where potatoes and egg are one of the key ingredients.
  • Hyderabadi Biryani - Here marinated chicken is cooked along with the rice with the flavor of saffron .
  • Mughlai Biryani - It is a biryani where chicken is cooked with curd, dried fruits, almond paste, ghee and green chilies .

Things that make chicken biryani so tasty:

Biryani is no doubt a delicious food with lots of flavors that also comes with several benefits. Whether you make biryani at home or order it at a restaurant, it will always satisfy your desire for delicious food. The cooking process of chicken biryani is nothing short of a culinary art, and every biryani lover should be aware of what goes into this dish to make it so wonderful.

Ingredients

  • The Chicken Marinade
    • Chicken: 2 lbs (approx. 1 kg), bone-in pieces prefered (drumsticks and thighs stay most succulent)
    • Yogurt: 1 cup, plain whole-milk yogurt (thick or Greek style)
    • Ginger-Garlic Paste: 2 tablespoons
    • Chili Powder: 1 to 2 teaspoons (Kashmiri chili powder gives a beautiful red color without burning heat)
    • Turmeric Powder: 1/2 teaspoon
    • Garam Masala: 1 teaspoon
    • Lemon Juice: 1 tablespoon
    • Salt: 1 to 1.5 teaspoons (to taste)
  • The Spiced Rice
    • Basmati Rice: 3 cups, high-quality extra-long grain (washed and soaked in water for 30 minutes)
    • Whole Spices:2-3 Green cardamoms, 1 Black cardamom, 3-4 Whole cloves, 1-inch Cinnamon stick, 1-2 Bay leaves
    • Salt: 2 tablespoons (The water should taste like seawater so the rice absorbs enough salt while boiling)
    • Oil or Ghee: 1 teaspoon (keeps the grains from sticking together)
  • The Fried Onions & Base Aromatics
    • Onions: 3 large, very thinly sliced
    • Ghee or Vegetable Oil: 1/2 cup (for frying the onions and cooking)
    • Green Chilies: 3-4, slit lengthwise (adjust for heat)
    • Fresh Mint Leaves: 1/2 cup, chopped
    • Fresh Cilantro (Coriander): 1/2 cup, chopped
  • The Layering & Saffron Finish
    • Saffron Strands: A generous pinch (~15-20 strands)
    • Warm Milk: 1/4 cup (to soak the saffron strands until the milk turns golden)
    • Ghee: 1-2 tablespoons (drizzled over the top layer for richness)
    • Kewra Water or Rose Water: 1 teaspoon (optional, for an authentic royal aroma)

Prepration Method

  1. Marinate the Chicken (30 mins to overnight):

    In a large bowl, combine the chicken, yogurt, ginger-garlic paste, chili powder, turmeric, garam masala, lemon juice, and salt. Mix thoroughly, ensuring the chicken is evenly coated. Cover and refrigerate for at least 30 minutes (overnight yields the best flavor).

  2. Fry the Onions (Birista) (12-15 mins):

    Heat the ghee or vegetable oil in a heavy-bottomed pot over medium heat. Add the sliced onions and fry, stirring frequently, until they turn an even golden-brown. Remove them immediately with a slotted spoon and spread them out on paper towels to crisp up. Do not let them turn dark brown or they will taste bitter.

  3. Cook the Chicken Base (20 mins):

    In the same pot with the remaining oil/ghee, add the marinated chicken and slit green chilies. Cook over medium-high heat for 5 minutes until the chicken changes color. Lower the heat, cover, and let it simmer for 15 minutes until the chicken is cooked through and a thick gravy forms. Stir in half of your fried onions, half of the mint, and half of the cilantro. Turn off the heat.

  4. Par-Boil the Rice (7-8 mins):

    While the chicken cooks, bring a large pot of water to a rolling boil with the whole spices (cardamom, cloves, cinnamon, bay leaves), 2 tbsp salt, and 1 tsp oil. Add the soaked basmati rice. Cook for exactly 7-8 minutes. Taste a grain: it should feel soft on the outside but still have a firm, distinct bite in the center (about 75% cooked). Drain completely.

  5. Layer the Biryani (5 mins):

    Take your heavy-bottomed pot with the chicken gravy at the bottom. Spread the drained, hot rice evenly over the chicken. Scatter the remaining fried onions, mint, and cilantro over the rice. Drizzle the saffron-infused milk, 1-2 tbsp ghee, and kewra/rose water evenly across the top to create those signature white and yellow rice patches.

  6. The Dum (Steam Cook) (20 mins):

    Seal the pot tightly to trap the steam. You can cover the pot with aluminum foil and then press the lid down firmly, or place a heavy weight on the lid. Cook on very low heat for 15-20 minutes. If your pot has a thin base, place a flat iron skillet (tawa) underneath it to prevent the bottom layer from scorching.
